Australian Tourism Dashboard: Trends and Insights

Tourism plays a vital role in the Australian economy, attracting millions of visitors every year from across the globe. The following visualizations provide an in-depth look at tourism patterns in Australia. By exploring data on short-term visitor arrivals by states, the number of tourists from various countries, and the long-term trends in overseas arrivals and departures, we can gain a deeper understanding of Australia's place in global tourism and how the industry has evolved over time.

Short-term Visitor Arrivals by Australian States

This map highlights the distribution of short-term visitor arrivals across Australian states in July 2024. Major tourist destinations like New South Wales, Queensland, and Victoria continue to attract the highest number of visitors, while less populated states see fewer arrivals. Understanding these patterns helps guide resource allocation and tourism development efforts across the country.

Number of Tourists per Country

The bar chart below illustrates the number of tourists visiting Australia from various countries, with an option to filter by year. By exploring this data, we can observe the shifting global tourism patterns, such as how events like international relations or pandemics affect tourist inflows from particular regions.

Annual Tourist Arrivals by Country (2019-2024)

This world map provides a global perspective on tourist arrivals to Australia from different countries over the years. The map allows for visual comparison between regions, showing the international reach of Australia's tourism industry and identifying key source markets.

Total Overseas Arrivals and Departures

The line chart tracks Australia's overseas arrivals and departures from 2014 to 2024. The sharp drop in 2020 corresponds to the global COVID-19 pandemic, where international travel was halted. As the economy rebounds, we see a gradual recovery in tourism starting from 2021, illustrating the resilience of the travel industry.
